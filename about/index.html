About

Marquez "Mark" Ocampo Go is a prominent businessman and politician serving as the incumbent representative for the lone legislative district of Baguio City. 

 
Mark Go has a strong educational background, having completed his AB Political Science in 1975 and a Master of Management in 1979, both from the University of the Philippines.   

His political career in Baguio City began with a bid for mayor in 2010, followed by a run for congressman in 2013. He was successfully elected as the representative for Baguio City in 2016 and has since been re-elected, serving multiple consecutive terms in the House of Representatives.

Throughout his tenure as congressman, Mark Go has been involved in authoring and co-authoring several significant pieces of legislation. Some notable examples include his work on bills that became Republic Act 10931 (Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education), Republic Act 11036 (National Mental Health Act), Republic Act 11084 and Republic Act 11889 (Increasing the bed capacity of Baguio General Hospital and Medical Center), and Republic Act 11223 (Universal Health Care Act of 2019). He has also championed local initiatives such as the proposed Baguio, La Trinidad, Itogon, Sablan, Tuba, and Tublay Development Authority (BLISTTDA) and the preservation of Burnham Park and Kennon Road as heritage zones.

Prior to his being Baguio's representative in congress, Mark Go was recognized as an Outstanding Citizen of Baguio in 1998. He has also received awards for his involvement with Rotary International, including Outstanding Rotary Club President in 1994 and Outstanding Rotary Governor (Worldwide) in 2002, as well as the Service Above Self award in 2012. In 2015, he was also recognized with the Distinguished Alumni Service Award by the UP Alumni Association.
